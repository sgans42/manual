How To Share a Word Document Through Email

Definition

Sending documents with others is an important process of collaboration and transferring ideas and information. Sending via email is a very quick and effective way to allow access of files and documents to colleagues, clients, employers, professors, etc. To get to sending documents via email, saving documents is vital to store and organize information so it does not get lost or erased. Before you can send information you must save it in case troubles arise that could delete the files.

Description

What is a Word Doc?
A Word Doc is a DOC file saved and created through the Office 365 application: Word. The .doc file is a filename extension used for word processing documents. A .docx is a broader file, similar to .doc, that can be opened by Microsoft Word, Apple Pages, OpenOffice, or GoogleDocs. What is email? Email is the distribution of messages from one computer or other electronic device to another via a network. What is sharing? Sharing virtual documents refers to “a digital file or document that can be assessed by specific users for viewing, downloading, or making changes to.” Sharing is different from sending because sending is just to “convey or transfer”, where the sending party no longer has access to the material sent. Why share? Many people share their documents in collaboration efforts with their colleagues, employers, or professors to see edits and changes made to documents while also adding notes and remarks regarding information in the document. Sharing is needed when two or more parties need to see each others’ work in real time.


What is email?


Email is the distribution of messages from one computer or other electronic device to another via a network. There are different types of email addresses and companies through which to send emails: Gmail (Google), iCloud (Apple), Outlook (Office), and Yahoo are the most common.

What is sending?


Sending is to convey or transfer documents or information. The sending party no longer has access to the item sent and changes cannot be seen in real time. The other option is sharing. Sharing is when a digital file or document can be assessed by specific recipients for viewing, downloading, or making changes to. The sender can see these changes in real time and make some of their own.

Why send?


Most of the time in business or educational scenarios, sending is what is really needed, but the senders share the information. Sending essentially removes one of the parties from the equation, and the receiving party can now gather whatever information they need and be able to send the information to another party without the mess of multiple users making changes to the same document at once.
  1. Open the Word application

  2. Open an existing document of choice or create a new document (you wish to share)
  3. When the document is completed:
    a. look to the upper left on the toolbar and click on ‘file’

    b. ‘save as’

    c. choose a location to save the file (a folder, desktop, external storage device, OneDrive, etc.

    d. enter the desired name of the document

    e. click ‘save’

    f. close Word
  1. Open your email
  2. Click on ‘new mail’ or ‘compose’
  3. Enter the email address of the desired recipient
  4. Fill out the subject line
  5. Fill out any more information that you wish to send to the recipient other than the document
  6. After the rest of the email is complete, click on the paperclip icon labeled ‘attach’
  7. Find the location of the saved file from step 6/C and select the desired document
  8. Review the email to make sure everything is complete, including checking the bottom of the email to ensure the document has been attached
  9. When finished reviewing, click on the ‘send’ button
